Wall arts can add character

Wall art can add character and personality to any space, whether it's a home, office, or public area. By choosing art pieces that resonate with you, you can create an atmosphere that reflects your individual taste and style. Wall art comes in many different forms, including paintings, prints, posters, and photographs, and can be used to create a focal point, add color and texture, or simply create a warm and inviting atmosphere. Additionally, displaying original art pieces can add value to your space and serve as a conversation starter. With so many options available, it's easy to find the perfect piece of wall art to enhance any space and make it truly your own.

Whimsy is always welcome

Whimsy is always welcome when it comes to adding personality and charm to any space. Whether it's a quirky art piece, a playful accent, or a colorful accessory, a touch of whimsy can bring a sense of joy and lightheartedness to your environment. Don't be afraid to let your imagination run wild and incorporate elements that make you smile and feel happy. After all, a space that feels welcoming and fun is sure to lift your mood and inspire creativity. So, embrace the whimsical side of decorating and let your personality shine through in your space.

Symmetry isn't your only friend

Symmetry may be a classic design principle, but it's not the only friend you should have when it comes to decorating walls. In fact, breaking away from strict symmetry can add visual interest and create a more dynamic and unexpected look. Asymmetrical displays, such as a gallery wall with a mix of sizes and shapes, can make a bold statement and add character to your space. Additionally, experimenting with different layouts, such as staggering frames or arranging them in a non-linear pattern, can add a sense of movement and flow. So, don't be afraid to break free from the traditional symmetrical approach and explore new ways to display your wall art and decor.

Metals are trendy

We live in a built environment that is varied and diverse, but increasingly, metal architecture is becoming more prevalent and influential. Chalk it up to modern, contemporary or post-contemporary aesthetics, but a significant portion of our architecture now is either substantially metal or heavily accented with metal. Metals have become a popular trend in modern home design, adding a sleek and stylish touch to houses. Metals can be used in a variety of ways, from hardware to light fixtures to decorative accents, and come in many different finishes, including brass, copper, chrome, and stainless steel. Using metal accents can bring a modern and industrial feel to any space, while also adding durability and longevity to your decor. Additionally, metal finishes can be paired with other materials, such as wood and stone, to create a dynamic and balanced look. So, whether you're looking to add a touch of sophistication or create a bold statement, incorporating metals into your home design is a trendy and versatile choice.

Bring the room to life with lightning

Lighting is an essential element in bringing a room to life and creating a mood or atmosphere. The right lighting can transform a space, making it feel warm and inviting, or bright and energizing. There are many ways to add lighting to a room, from overhead fixtures to table lamps to floor lamps. Consider the function of the room and the mood you want to create when selecting lighting. For example, warm, dim lighting can create a cozy and intimate atmosphere in a bedroom, while bright and functional lighting is important in a home office or kitchen. Additionally, using a mix of different lighting sources can add depth and texture to a space. So, whether you're looking to create a relaxing sanctuary or a vibrant entertaining area, lighting is a key factor in bringing your room to life.

Don't underestimate curtains

Curtains are an often-underestimated element in home decor, but they can make a big impact on the overall look and feel of a room. Curtains can add color, texture, and depth to a space, while also providing privacy and controlling natural light. When choosing curtains, consider the style and mood you want to create in the room, as well as the colors and patterns that will complement the other elements in the space. Additionally, the length and style of the curtains can also play a role in the overall effect. Long, flowing curtains can add drama and elegance, while shorter, more tailored curtains can create a more casual and modern look. So, don't underestimate the power of curtains to transform your space and add a finishing touch to your decor.
